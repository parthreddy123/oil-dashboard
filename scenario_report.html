Built in a single sprint through iterative collaboration between a strategist and Claude Code (AI pair-programmer). Each phase shows the human direction and what shipped. From first prompt to live deployment — one working session.

Phase 1 — The Brief
Scenario Engine for Oil Geopolitics
Build a scenario engine that analyzes Middle East crisis scenarios and estimates impact on crude oil prices and Indian refinery margins. Score real news articles against scenarios using AI.
  • Designed 5 geopolitical scenarios: Ceasefire, Quick Resolution, Regime Change, Prolonged Standoff, Conflagration
  • Built LLM pipeline scoring each news article against all 5 scenarios with signal strength [-1, +1] and reasoning
  • Implemented softmax probability computation from average signal strengths
  • Created Expected Value (EV) framework: probability-weighted Brent price and GRM across scenarios
  • Added strategic narrative generation — LLM synthesizes top signals into a concise briefing

Phase 3 — Price Grounding
From Ratios to Analyst Consensus
The Brent ratios don’t work — no way we’ll have $160. Why can’t we find internet consensus per scenario and hard-code per scenario? Also the product prices?
  • Initial approach used multiplicative ratios — produced unrealistic extremes ($160+ Brent)
  • Pivoted to additive premiums — still too crude for a strategy tool
  • Researched analyst consensus from Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, Morgan Stanley, Citi, Wood Mackenzie, Rystad Energy, Deutsche Bank, ING, CSIS, and Oxford Economics (March 2026)
  • Hardcoded scenario-conditional prices grounded in historical parallels (post-JCPOA, Abqaiq, Arab Spring, Gulf War)
  • Added per-scenario product prices from observed crisis crack spread data

Engine

LLM Scoring (Claude Haiku 4.5)
Article → 5 signals
Each article scored [-1, +1] against each scenario with reasoning
Softmax probabilities
avg_signal → exp(avg × temp) → normalize. Temperature=3
Expected Value
EV = Σ(probi × scenario_pricei) for oil, GRM, products
GRM Computation
Crude cost = Indian basket (72% Dubai + 28% Brent)
Product revenue = Σ(pricei × weighti)
