What is this? This page uses AI to analyze recent geopolitical news and estimate how different Middle East crisis scenarios could affect crude oil prices and refinery margins. Articles are scored by an LLM against 5 scenarios; probabilities are computed via softmax over average signal strengths. The Expected Value (EV) for each metric is the probability-weighted average across all scenarios — not a price forecast, but a risk-weighted expectation given current signals.
How is GRM calculated? Gross Refining Margin (GRM) measures the difference between the value of refined products (petrol, diesel, ATF, naphtha, fuel oil, LPG) and the cost of crude oil. We use a typical Indian refinery product slate: diesel 42%, petrol 22%, naphtha 12%, ATF 10%, fuel oil 8%, LPG 6%. Each scenario estimates how supply disruptions, crude cost changes, and demand shifts would affect this margin.
How are Brent prices per scenario estimated? Each scenario’s assumed Brent price is based on historical crisis parallels and analyst consensus: Ceasefire ($55-58) reflects pre-crisis normalization, similar to post-JCPOA 2015 levels. Prolonged Standoff ($95-100) reflects 2022-style sustained supply risk premium. Conflagration ($140-160) is based on the 1990 Gulf War spike and 2008 oil shock. These are not forecasts — they are scenario-conditional estimates grounded in historical precedent.
